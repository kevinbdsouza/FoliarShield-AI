# Responsible-Use Guidance

FoliarShield-AI outputs are research decision support artifacts for literature review,
experiment planning, and benchmark comparison. They are not direct foliar product,
microbial, pesticide, regulatory, or field-deployment recommendations.

## Required Output Framing

Candidate shortlists must include:

- evidence citations;
- uncertainty intervals or confidence notes;
- living-payload and biosafety flags;
- material and sprayability feasibility flags;
- proxy-to-target risk notes;
- release and retention assumptions;
- explicit research-only status.

## Guardrails

- Block candidate promotion when biosafety flags are present.
- Require contained-use approval before living-payload assays.
- Separate literature-supported claims from model-generated hypotheses.
- Preserve source licenses and provenance links.
- Require human review before greenhouse, polyhouse, or field-testing interpretation.
- Avoid claiming agronomic efficacy from proxy-only, fixture-only, or in silico scores.

## Review Triggers

Escalate a candidate for domain review when:

- living-payload identity or approval status is ambiguous;
- evidence is low confidence, single-source, or extraction-derived only;
- material compatibility or phytotoxicity evidence is indirect;
- release profile, rainfastness, or retention is inferred rather than measured;
- public release rights are unclear;
- a candidate would move from physical proxy assays to biological assays.
